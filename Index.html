Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Income

The data showcases states with lower levels of income also have the highest percentage of lack in healthcare. These states showing the highest levels of both criteria include Texas, Georgia and Mississippi. We can also see that 11 states are past the 17% threshold in poverty with most states having a disparity in healthcare mostly taking place in southern states. Most of the west coast states hover in the middle of the pack for both categories. We have 12 states below 12% poverty, all displaying lower levels of lack of healthercare. In conclusion the two factors do show a positive correlation.
